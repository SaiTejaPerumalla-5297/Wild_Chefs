💡 Reflections

Understanding Branches

I explored how branches help in parallel development.

Q: Can you explain what a branch is in version control systems like Git?

A: A branch in Git is a lightweight movable pointer to a commit. It lets developers work on features or fixes separately from the main codebase.

CLI Shortcuts

I discovered helpful keyboard shortcuts to speed up my workflow in the terminal.

Q: What are some keyboard shortcuts you found most useful in the terminal?

A: Ctrl + A (start of line), Ctrl + E (end of line), Ctrl + R (search history), and Ctrl + C (cancel process) help me work efficiently.

Working in a Team

I learned how important communication and coordination are in group development.

Q: What did you learn from collaborating with your team?

A: I realized version control and regular check-ins reduce confusion and keep everyone on track during development.

Kent Beck's Empirical Software Design

I learned about Kent Beck's Empirical Software Design, which focuses on building software step by step, learning from real-world feedback.

Q: What does Kent Beck’s Empirical Software Design focus on?

A: It emphasizes making small changes, testing them, and improving the design based on what works in real-world usage.

🌾 Real-Time Indian Foods You Can Cook

🥞 Masala Dosa

📍 Origin: Karnataka, South India
📖 Description: Thin, crispy rice-lentil crepe with spiced potato filling.
🍽 Served with: Coconut chutney, sambar, tomato chutney.
⏱ Why it’s real-time: Always made fresh on a hot griddle for crispiness!

🍛 Chole Bhature

📍 Origin: Punjab/North India
📖 Description: Spicy chickpea curry with fluffy deep-fried bread.
🍽 Served with: Pickle, onions, green chutney.
⏱ Why it’s real-time: Bhature are puffed hot and fresh; chole is slow-cooked to perfection.

🍞 Pav Bhaji

📍 Origin: Mumbai, Maharashtra
📖 Description: Spicy veggie mash served with butter-toasted buns.
🍽 Served with: Onions, lemon wedge, extra butter.
⏱ Why it’s real-time: Constantly mashed on a hot skillet and served piping hot.
